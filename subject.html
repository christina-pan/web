    Se7en

    David Fincher's second feature film--the gritty psychological thriller Se7en-- is set place in a nondescript city as two detectives, Somerset and Mills, solve crimes of a serial killer who bases his crimes off the seven deadly sins. Woefully nihilistic and sinister in both its masterful cinematography and characterization, Se7en has become sort of a cult classic over the years. It's simultaneously terrifying and absolutely thrilling--all the more astounding considering the online onscreen murder is done by a cop. The iconic line of "What's in the box" will likely stay in your head (HA!) for a very long time.

    Zodiac

    David Fincher's sixth feature film--the investigative psychological thriller Zodiac--is a dramatization of the investigation of the Zodiac killer. Equally terrifying is the Zodiac's unspeakable crimes and their flirtation with the San Francisco Globe newspaper; as so much is the obsession across decades of select individuals and institution failure brought upon the criminal justice system, and the lingering knowledge today that the perpetrator behind these murders has never been fully identified.

    Gone Girl

    David Fincher's tenth feature film is based off Gillian Flynn's bestelling novel under the synonymous name. We follow the ongoing investigation of the missing Amy Elliot Dunne, and we follow her husband, Nick Dunne; through it all we uncover a deeper, endlessly sinister underbelly of a couple's relationship and the twisted psychosis of both individuals through a larger critique of mass media, a patriarchal society, and perhaps most irreverently, the "Cool Girl."
